Question

Our experiment aims to answer what type of shampoo is the most effective for our hair.

What is the most efficient type of shampoo: natural, partly natural, or artificial?

Hypothesis

My teammate Yashi and I predicted that the partly natural shampoo will perform most efficiently.

This is because partly natural shampoo has the benefit of both natural and aritifical substances.

Observation

We observed 4 different experiments for each of our shampoos to test their efficiency.

Coconut Shampoo (natural): succeeded 1/4 tests, the pH test

Castile Soap Shampoo (partly-natural): performed average, failed only the pH test

Store-Bought Soap Shampoo (artificial): succeeded 3/4 test, failed only the pH test

Experiment

#1. Prepare 5g of wool pieces to simulate it as hair

#2. Create a sebum to simulate it as dirt

#3. Prepare 3 kinds of shampoos: Coconut, Castile, and Artificial

#4. Using a blank ink, test the cleaning power of each shampoos

#5. Test the pH of each shampoos to know the compatibility with our skin

#6. Using the sebum, test the grease removal of each shampoos

#7. With a graduated cylinder, test the foaming level of each shampoos

We also 3D printed our own shampoo bottles!

Data

Conclusion

Artifical shampoo is the most effective type of shampoo for our hair,

since it has the right cleaning power, highest grease removal ability, and highest foam level.

Our hypothesis was partially correct, since the castile soap shammpoo performed average.
